Startup Business Case
Company Name Lwandle Protection Services
Date 1 March 2026
1. Market Problem
Pain point: Public safety services are viewed as unreliable and under resourced which leads
to many South Africans to turn to private protection providers.
South Africa has fewer police officers relative to its high population, which causes many
South Africans to seek private protection. Recent surveys show that only 22% of South
Africans trust the public protection services (Police) while 62% of South Africans have been
reported to distrust the police which is a clear sign that private protection services are
preferred.
2. Product / Service Overview
We offer high-quality, fully complaint protection services which includes (VIP,
armed/unarmed guarding, rapid response, event security, and consulting) with a focus on
professional training, legal compliance, (PSIRA), and technology-enabled operations.
What makes our company better than current solutions is that we provide consistent
standard and lawful protection, officers are skilled and continuously upskilled, accessible
options for different client needs, real-time reporting and GPS tracking, and our managers
are dedicated and plans are personalized.
3. Target Market
The core customers are businesses, estates, event organizers, and VIP individuals situated in
major cities like Durban, Johannesburg, and Cape Town. They seek reliable, fast, and visible
security services due to high crime levels and safety concerns.
4. Business Model
Revenue will come from guarding, VIP protection, event security, alarm response,
consulting, training, and specialized security services.
5. Go-To Market Strategy
Digital ads: Targeted campaigns on platforms like Facebook, Instagram, and Google.
Partnerships: Collaborating with local businesses or organizations with our targeted
audiences.
Direct Sales: Personalized emails, calls, and community events.
User acquisition tactics
We can offer promotions, or free trials to attract early customers.
We can implement referral programs to leverage word-of-mouth.
We can use testimonials and social proof to establish trust.
